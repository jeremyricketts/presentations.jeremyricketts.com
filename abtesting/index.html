Example 1: AB Testing A Homepage design.

"2012-09-18 Homepage Pieces"

Question: What pieces of our current design are important to visitors?

The control (no change) 5.7% CR

Variation A (no Facebook bar) 5.8% CR

Variation B (no content at all) 6.2% CR

Possible lessons:

  • Less content is good.
  • That particular content stinks.
  • The design of that content stinks.

Example 2: AB Test Checkout Change.

"2012-09-11 Helper List Badges"

Question: Do customers want more fine-grain filters for selecting a mover?

The control (no change) 3.5% CR

Variation A (new badges) 3.3% CR

This test was inconclusive.

It ran for months and 52,002 people were tested.

But… there was an increase in customers calling in with questions. (We tracked phone calls.)

Possible lessons:

  • More choices is confusing.
  • Taking focus away from customer reviews is bad.
  • Raising quality questions causes customers to call.

Sales aren't the only thing worth tracking.

  • Returns.
  • Customer service burden.
  • Customer satisfaction metrics.

Off the shelf AB testing services suck at this.

Big Lessons

#1:Document Tests

Pose hypothesis, document results & potential lessons.

You'll start to see patterns and connections you didn't see before.

Example: "Helper List"

#2:Test One Thing At A Time

This is very hard to do. But you have to isolate the variables as much as possible.

#3:Decide your level of statistical significance.

… and don't peek. (great article)

#4:Don't Test Everything

Learn lessons and move on. Don't test big obvious things.

  • Is 30% of your traffic mobile? Don't test a design that's easier to read on mobile.
  • No need to test lowering prices. Duh.

#5:Don't Just Iterate On What You Have

QA and user test first. Look for larger issues:

  • Confused users.
  • Erratic visitor paths
  • Inbound keywords that are all over the map
  • High bounce rate

… these indicate a larger problem.

#6:Test BIG Ideas

Sure you can eek out some %'s by changing the color of buttons.

It takes forever. Instead, go for the heavy hitters.
